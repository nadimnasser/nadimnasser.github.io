About Hafeez



Hafeez is a second generation Canadian born in the Thorncliffe Park neighbourhood in a family of South Asian and East African origin and grew up in Markham. Hafeez attended high school at the University of Toronto Schools and completed his undergraduate studies at McMaster University with a Bachelor of Science. While growing up, he and his family had extensive exposure to hospitals and the healthcare system. Given these interactions from an early age, Hafeez was drawn to understanding how healthcare systems could be improved to serve all Canadians in a high-quality, equitable and affordable way, and pursued a Master of Public Health at Columbia University in New York City.

While studying at Columbia, Hafeez focused on understanding how disparities in healthcare access affected the most underprivileged communities globally, which led him to pursue an early career in global public health, working across Sub-Saharan African and Asia. He started his career working with communities in South Africa to identify innovative products to prevent the transmission of HIV in vulnerable, female populations by over 50%.

He continued fighting for access to healthcare for under-served populations with the Clinton Health Access Initiative, where his focus was to make medicines more affordable. He also worked closely with governments across Africa and Asia, as well as vaccine manufacturers to design, develop and distribute vaccines across both continents.

Although passionate about serving these communities, Hafeez grew frustrated with the inefficiencies in global health systems and decided to take a different approach to supporting low- income communities: by using investments in job creation to create economic resiliency. He pursued a Master of Business Administration at the Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania and joined the global management consulting company, Bain & Company, working to support the growth of companies and investment firms across the US and Canada.

Informed by these experiences, Hafeez has spent the past few years supporting investments in health, education and economic opportunity in Africa, Asia and North America by serving as an advisor to the World Bank and other social investors. He currently serves as a Founding Director of Sustained Impact Partners, an advisory firm working with investors in the UN's Sustainable Development Goals and as a Co-Founder of SemperConnect, a Canadian organization supporting Canadian seniors to access high-quality care to age independently at-home. Hafeez is passionate about increasing economic opportunity across Canada and among disenfranchised populations globally, and recently published an article advocating for the introduction of a universal basic income in Canada.

Hafeez lives with his brilliant and incredibly tolerant wife, Sabrina, in Toronto and enjoys spending his weekends jogging around the city, consuming all things cheddar from numerous farmers’ markets or attempting his hand, unsuccessfully, at stand-up comedy.
